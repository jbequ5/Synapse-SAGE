# Synapse / SAGE Long-Term Vision & Strategic Ideas (Living Document)

## Core Philosophy
We are building a self-referential, vector-first intelligence layer that compounds across every EM instance in the SAGE swarm. The system must optimize five balanced objectives at all times:
- implementation_quality
- prediction_accuracy
- value_creation
- learning_to_learn
- robustness

No objective may be sacrificed for short-term gains. Red-teaming is non-negotiable at every stage.

## High-Level Product Directions
1. **Distilled Enigma Models** — Compact, locally-runnable models that outperform larger models on EM-specific tasks through continuous distillation from swarm telemetry.
2. **Autonomous Economic Layer** — Products that generate real revenue while feeding value back into the intelligence flywheel.
3. **HyperAgent Co-Pilot** — Real-time, context-aware assistant that can trigger KAS hunts, Meta-RL proposals, or full daily cycles on demand.
4. **Swarm-Wide Reflection Engine** — The system continuously writes its own history and learns from its past decisions via reflection_log.md.

## Research & Experiment Ideas (Prioritized by Weakest Objective Impact)
- Develop a learned meta-optimizer that evolves the five objective weights over time.
- Experiment with multi-agent red-teaming (multiple specialized red-team agents).
- Investigate vector-based novelty detection to prevent knowledge collapse in KAS.
- Explore hybrid symbolic + neural approaches inside the student model for verifier-first reasoning.

## Success Criteria (Measurable)
- Average vector strength across all five objectives > 0.85 sustained over 30 days.
- Distilled models show measurable EFS lift in blind EM runs.
- System can identify and autonomously fix its own weakest objective without human intervention.
